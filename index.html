Where this comes from

I started on a commercial vehicle trim line at Tata Motors — production management, quality programmes, PMO. Then three roles at Mahindra's tractor business: building a CLTV model on 2M+ customers, owning an $80M brand P&L, and designing the channel strategy for a newly acquired implements business.

At Monitor Deloitte, I led pricing transformations, GTM strategies, and post-M&A commercial architecture redesigns across automotive, tech, retail, and media — including a $50M pricing transformation for one of the largest US auto parts retailers and Cisco's XaaS transition.

At Accenture Technology Strategy, I led EA rationalization for a global automotive OEM (~10K applications, 2,500+ capabilities), and sit on the core team driving agentic AI adoption across Strategy CoEs.

The exploration

The work I've done across consulting engagements is structurally similar to what senior PM roles demand — understanding a complex problem space, framing the right question, designing a solution that a cross-functional team can execute, and governing the outcomes. The vocabulary is different. The underlying method is the same.

AI is changing the equation. The tools barrier — historically the moat between strategy and product — is narrowing. What remains is the upstream skill: knowing which problem to solve, for whom, and why now. That is consulting-trained.

This public portfolio is the bridge: demonstrating that the competency already operates at the required level, while building the domain fluency, technical vocabulary, and community credibility that tech-first product roles require.

The structural shift

The traditional barrier between strategy and execution — knowing how to build the thing — is narrowing faster than most institutions expect. AI is abstracting the tool-specific layer, which amplifies the upstream skills that were always the hard part.

01
Synthesising ambiguous signals into a crisp problem statement
No LLM finds the right question on its own. Translating messy stakeholder input into a defensible hypothesis — and knowing when the brief is wrong — remains firmly human. Twelve years of consulting is the training ground.
Consulting core · irreplaceable
02
Generating, evaluating, and iterating on solutions at speed
AI dramatically accelerates the solution generation and evaluation loop. A strategist who can direct AI agents to prototype, test, and refine operates faster than a specialist who owns only one step of that process.
AI-amplified · already in practice
03
Cross-functional alignment — stakeholders who don't share incentives
C-suite stakeholder management, sequencing decisions across teams without a shared reporting line, governing outcomes over an 18-month delivery cycle. This is what consulting at scale looks like. It is also what senior delivery roles look like.
Domain-agnostic · proven at manager level
04
Technical depth and tooling — the narrowing moat
Historically the barrier for specialists. Still relevant — but the gap between "knows which question to ask of a system" and "built the system from scratch" is closing. The focus shifts to the judgment layer above the tools.
Barrier lowering · actively bridging
Building in Public

Most career explorations are announced after the fact. I'm doing the opposite — publishing real, verifiable artefacts in the open across five strategic domains over 50 weeks. Not retrospective case studies. Not opinion pieces. Actual frameworks, analyses, and interactive tools — each scoped and shipped like a product. The goal is dual: to demonstrate that my thinking already operates at the required level, and to deliberately build the domain fluency and community credibility of the tech product world I'm moving toward.

Demonstrating

  • Problem framing and solution design that operates upstream of sprint execution
  • Commercial strategy depth — pricing, GTM, channel, operating model — translated into reusable tools
  • Data-informed decisions: ML, analytics, and causal inference frameworks for strategy practitioners
  • Independent end-to-end shipping: discovery → architecture → live public artefact

Learning

  • Tech PM practices at product-led companies, outside large-enterprise consulting delivery models
  • AI product strategy: from adoption advisory to roadmap ownership for AI-native features
  • Consumer and B2B discovery methods versus enterprise stakeholder management
  • Building audience, credibility, and feedback loops through sustained public work
